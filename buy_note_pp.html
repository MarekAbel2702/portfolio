Privacy Policy – BuyNote

Last updated: May 16, 2026

Your privacy is very important to us. This Privacy Policy explains what data is processed when using the BuyNote application and how we protect user privacy.

1. What data do we collect?

The application does not collect any personal data from users. We do not gather, store, sell, transfer, or process any information that could directly identify the user.

BuyNote does not require user registration, login, account creation, email address, phone number, or any personal profile information.

2. Shopping lists and app data

BuyNote allows users to create and manage shopping lists. The user may add data such as:

  • shopping list names,
  • product names,
  • product quantities,
  • information whether a product has been marked as bought,
  • selected application language.

This data is processed and stored exclusively locally on the user’s device. It is not uploaded to any server, not stored online, not transmitted over the internet, and not shared with third parties.

3. Local storage

BuyNote may store shopping lists and application settings locally on the device in order to keep the user’s data available after the application is closed and reopened.

Locally stored data may include:

  • created shopping lists,
  • products added to lists,
  • product quantities,
  • bought / not bought status,
  • selected app language.

This data remains on the user’s device and is not accessible to us. We do not have access to the shopping lists or products created by the user.

4. User preferences

The application may store basic preferences locally on the device, such as:

  • selected app language,
  • interface-related settings.

These preferences are stored only on the user’s device and are not sent to us or to any third party.

5. Ads and analytics

The application:

  • does not display ads,
  • does not use analytics tools,
  • does not integrate third-party tracking services,
  • does not track users,
  • does not use advertising identifiers.

6. Internet connection

BuyNote does not require an internet connection for its core functionality. Shopping lists are created, edited, saved, and loaded locally on the user’s device.

The application does not send shopping list data, product names, quantities, or language preferences to any external server.

7. App permissions

BuyNote is designed to work without requesting sensitive device permissions. The application does not require access to camera, microphone, contacts, location, messages, photos, media library, or other unrelated system data.

The application does not access:

  • contacts,
  • camera,
  • microphone,
  • precise or approximate location,
  • photos or media library,
  • messages,
  • calendar,
  • call logs,
  • or any other unrelated personal data.

8. Sharing data with third parties

The application does not share any personal data with third parties, because it does not collect any personal data.

Shopping lists and app preferences remain stored locally on the user’s device.

9. Data deletion

Users can delete their shopping list data directly inside the application by removing lists or products.

Users can also remove all locally stored application data by uninstalling the application or clearing the application data in the device system settings.

10. Security

BuyNote minimizes privacy risks by keeping user data locally on the device. Since the application does not transmit shopping list data over the internet, the risk of external interception or server-side data breach is significantly reduced.

Users are responsible for securing access to their own device using available device security features such as screen lock, PIN, password, fingerprint, or face unlock.

11. Children’s privacy

The application is not specifically intended for children and does not knowingly collect any personal data from minors.

12. Changes to this Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy occasionally. Any new version will be published with an updated date.

Continued use of the application after changes to this Privacy Policy means that the user accepts the updated version.
